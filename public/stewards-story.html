In racing, when something needs explaining, the stewards call an inquiry. They pull the evidence, they hear the argument, and they publish a verdict even when it is unflattering. This is that, but for a betting model.

01

The problem

Betting on horses is easy. Knowing whether you are any good at it is almost impossible. A punter remembers the winners, forgets the losers, and tells themselves a story that flatters. Memory is a terrible auditor.

The Stewards' Room exists to make that self-deception impossible. Every bet is logged before the race runs, with the reasoning attached, and settled against the official record afterwards. Not the version you remember. The version that happened.

02

The charter

Rule one

Write the reason down first

Every leg carries the reasoning it was picked on, recorded before the result is known. You cannot retro-fit a good story onto a lucky win if the story is already in writing.

Rule two

Flat stakes. Always.

Early on, the model staked more on the picks it felt best about. Those picks did worst. The confidence label now records what the model expected and moves no money at all. It is an honesty meter, not a throttle.

Rule three

Prove it before you scale it

No new idea gets more money until it has earned it over a real sample. Every trial runs capped, in its own ledger, so a bad idea costs almost nothing to discover.

Rule four

Reconcile to the cent

The journal is checked against the actual Singapore Pools receipts, every day. If the wallet and the ledger disagree, the ledger is wrong until proven otherwise.

04

What we actually learned

Finding

The place pool is the quiet hero

96 of 156 place bets returned money. A 61.5% cash rate. Backing a horse to merely run top-three is unglamorous and it is the thing that has kept the book alive.

Finding

Confidence was pointing backwards

The picks labelled High confidence performed worst of any band. Not slightly, consistently. That discovery is the single most valuable thing the journal has produced, and it only surfaced because the labels were being written down and scored.

Most punters never find this out about themselves. This one is measured every single day.

Finding

South Africa is the sharp end

Across 86 legs, the South African book is running at -0.2%. Effectively breakeven, in a game engineered to take a cut of every ticket. It is the region with the best data, and it shows.

Improvement

The tool keeps auditing itself

The engine now re-tests its own rules against the full ledger, so a rule that stops being true announces itself instead of quietly being inherited. It has already caught one that had gone stale.

The source list has been rebuilt too, from 24 to 43 verified feeds across every region on the coupon.

Singapore Pools takes up to 18% out of every pool before a single dividend is paid. That is the tide everyone swims against. Across 274 settled bets, the model swims about four points faster than it.

It is not a profit. It was never going to be one on a coffee-money stake. But it is a measurable, honest edge over the takeout, and it is written down where it cannot lie.
07

Set for August

A target has to be something you can be caught missing. "Make a profit" is a wish, and on 27 days of data it would take roughly 500 more bets to even prove it happened. So the targets are things that can be counted by the 31st.

01

Zero bets at meets we cannot read

The inquiry's sharpest finding. Mauritius and Japan — the two regions with no form data available — account for 7% of the bets and 35% of the entire loss. Twenty legs, running at -64.5%, against -10.0% everywhere else. Skip those and the book is a different animal. Countable: the number should be nil.

02

Get Medium-High to 100 settled bets

It sits at 46. Not enough to trust either way — the honest read needs closer to 200. Reaching 100 by the end of August would put the band halfway to a verdict that means something, instead of a hunch wearing a number.

03

Keep the ledger at zero flags

Fourteen betting days, 292 bets, reconciled to the cent against the official receipts, with nothing unexplained. That is the part that makes every other number on this page worth reading. It stays at zero.

04

Hold the line on stakes

No sizing up after a good day. The one time the model bet more on its favourite picks, those picks did worst. $10 and $5, flat, regardless of how good it feels. The discipline is the asset.

Most people bet for a month and end up with a feeling.
This one ended up with evidence.
