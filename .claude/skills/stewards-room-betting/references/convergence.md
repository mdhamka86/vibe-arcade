# The Convergence Grid

Convergence is the heart of the Outsider Method. It measures how strongly
independent expert opinions and data signals agree on a horse. High agreement
across genuinely independent sources is the signal; a single loud tipster is
not.

## Building the grid (reconstruct, don't skim)

For each meet analysed:

1. **Gather external opinion** from the region's sources (see `triage.md`).
   This is mandatory on every meet analysed, not just the ones you fancy.
2. **Reconstruct the full pundit grid** — every tip, every pundit, every form
   read (horses, jockeys, track, going, weather). Do not surface-skim; the
   whole point is the same depth you'd build by hand in chat.
3. **Layer in the data cross-check** — sectionals and pace maps — for structure
   that pure opinion misses.
4. **Classify each horse's convergence band.**

## Bands and what they mean

- **Medium-High** — the only solidly green band (~+19.2% ROI). This is where
  the book should be biased. Bet these.
- **Marginal (Medium / Low-Med)** — thin PLA insurance at most, and trim it.
  From the 26 June selection-bias rule, trim marginal Medium/Low-Med PLA spots
  rather than pad the book with them.
- **Low / longshot-driven** — usually the overbet-longshot trap. Fade or avoid.

## Confidence labels are NOT convergence

Keep them separate. Confidence labels are a calibration gauge only. History
shows label inversion (High underperforming Low-Med), so they never feed
staking. Convergence band feeds staking; confidence just tracks calibration.

## The exploitable edge, restated

Because SGPools is parimutuel, the money is in fading overbet longshots and
milking the place pool on well-converged runners, not in blindly backing
market favourites.
